The upper river

What's left of the 210-foot high Glines Canyon Dam, 30 feet remaining at the base to be blasted out and removed. In the distance is the former Lake Mills that forms the new Elwha Valley.
<%= t.include("_upper.html") %>

Big wood on the move

After the Lower Elwha dam was deconstructed, the Elwha River now runs freely through what was Lake Aldwell that has lots of woody debris and is now filling in with vegetation.
<%= t.include("_logs.html") %>

The middle river

A pair of nesting dippers pause very briefly while fishing underwater in an Elwha River tributary.
<%= t.include("_middle.html") %>

The lower river's resurgence

In this 2010 photo, taken before the dam was removed, adult Chinook salmon hit the end of the river in a pool below the Elwha Dam that blocks their swim upstream.
<%= t.include("_lower.html") %>

At the mouth of the Elwha

Now after years of sediment being released from the deconstruction of the Elwha and Glines Canyon Dam, the rivermouth of the Elwha is forming a giant beach for as far as you can see instead of cobble stones.
